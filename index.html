    Patron's Message

    When we look at the world scenario today we cannot avoid taking painful note of situations of violence of every type. It forces us to say that we built a society which is violent. Should we the privileged ones who saw the first light of the day this century not change the present situation of the world and make it a better world bringing in positive qualities of human beings - peace, unity, understanding and joy ?

    The best way we could bring about change in society is by bringing about change in our own personal life and outlook; by preparing oneself to accept the hardships of life; by replacing greed, envy, jealousy, hatred and despair with love, compassion and forgiveness; and by cultivating a deep faith in God who is the source of all good.

    I wish and pray that the dedicated ex-students of St. Fidelis College will be successful in making the way that the insight into the working of self is greater than academic learning. It's only then the knowledge will be channelized properly for it's expansion and enrichment. I call upon all ex-Fidelians to think big, hold your heads; high, you have talents. If no one else uses them, you yourself use them.

    Good Luck.


    Principal

    History of St. Fidelis

    Fidelis Roy, the patron of the college was born in South Western city of Germany in October 1578 in a noble family.  His father was the Mayor of the city.

    Fidelis as a young lad was very brilliant and had stupendous memory power and was awarded doctorate in Philosophy and Letters in 1601 at the age of 23 from the University of Freiberg, Germany.  Later he also studies law and received a doctorate in civil and church law.  He was soon in great demand because of his abilities. 

    He became the voice of the voiceless, the poor and those met with injustice.God also had a better plan for him and was calling him to a higher, more challenging, hard and dedicated life in the service of humanity and in doing so he died a martyr’s death living and remaining faithful to his calling, on 24th April, 1622.

    As per church tradition he was declared Saint (holy man) by Pope Benedict XIV in 1746.  As Fidelians we have to imbibe the spirit of St. Fidelis and do our work and duties in all sincerity, honesty and always serve the people in need.
